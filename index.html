Weight Maintenance After Loss

When talking about maintaining or losing weight, or gaining it for that matter, caloric intake never leaves the conversation. It is a simple fact of life, if you burn more calories than you consume, you will lose mass— and vice versa.

What is overlooked, however, is the difficulty of being in a caloric deficit without proper habit management. All the things that come with the journey to weight loss and maintaining your desired body weight are affected by the habits you practice everyday, both subconsciously and deliberately.

A good book to understand habit management and the effects the little things you do everyday have on your life as it incrementally piles up is “Atomic Habits” by James Clear.

Studying scholarly papers, we come to the conclusion that the only way you will lose mass, regardless of what fancy diet you come up with or decide to follow, in order to lose mass, you have to burn calories and consume less than you burn in order to be in a deficit. This becomes easy when your goals are clear to you and your space is organized in a way that your journey is almost seamless and everything just clicks into place due to the optimization you have curated after research.


  • Fatemeh Azizi Soeliman, et al.
    "Weight regain after weight loss is a common problem for all those obese or overweight who have had a recent weight loss. Different cures such as diet therapy, behavioral therapy, exercise or a mixture of them have been advised as solutions."

  • Stephanie Ramage, et al.
    "A comprehensive approach, including reduced dietary intake, regular physical activity, and behavioural strategies, is warranted and is supported by the research evidence."

  • Yiannis Koutras, et al.
    "Eating more frequently home cooked meals and less eating away from home meals may be beneficially associated with weight loss maintenance. WRBI seems to be a useful tool when dealing with patients who have previously lost significant weight."
